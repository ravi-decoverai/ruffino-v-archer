Key Disputes

Central Question

Did the failure to administer IV tPA and transfer John Ruffino to a comprehensive stroke center for endovascular thrombectomy on February 17, 2016, fall below the standard of care, and did this cause a worse neurological outcome?

Plaintiffs' Position

  • Mr. Ruffino was neurologically normal from at least 0949 through 1200 on February 17, 2016.
  • Any symptoms by 0830 were, at most, caused by a TIA (transient ischemic attack), not a stroke.
  • The severe stroke did not manifest until at or after 1200.
  • When Dr. Archer identified new neurological deficits at ~1220, these changes had been present less than one hour since the patient was last seen neurologically normal at 1200.
  • Dr. Archer was required to initiate IV tPA and arrange transfer to a comprehensive stroke center.
  • Dr. Archer and Nurse Bromley fell below the standard of care by not effectively communicating to Dr. Chitturi that Mr. Ruffino was neurologically normal from 1000-1200.
  • Had treatment been provided within 6-8 hours of 1220, Mr. Ruffino would have had a better outcome.
  • CT angiogram revealed a clot suitable for endovascular thrombectomy at a comprehensive stroke center.

Defendants' Position

  • Mr. Ruffino's left MCA was already chronically occluded or 99% stenosed since at least December 2015 (MRA evidence).
  • He woke up with symptoms on February 17, making "last known normal" when he went to bed the night before.
  • He was well beyond the 3-hour tPA window by the time he arrived at StoneCrest at 9:48 a.m.
  • Even if tPA had been given, it would not have opened a chronically occluded vessel.
  • His NIHSS score of 4 made him ineligible for endovascular thrombectomy per AHA/ASA guidelines (requires NIHSS >= 6).
  • The M1 segment had been completely occluded or significantly stenosed since at least December 23, 2015; thrombectomy and stenting were not appropriate.
  • The care at StoneCrest met the standard of care and was not the proximate cause of any injury.

Medical History

  • December 2015: Began experiencing intermittent transient neurological events (right arm numbness, heaviness, dizziness, sudden speech arrest).
  • First episode in WalMart with sudden speech arrest and right arm heaviness.
  • December 14, 2015: Referred to neurologist Dr. Deka Efobi by Dr. Luck. Diagnosed with TIAs, vertebrobasilar syndrome.

  • February 11, 2016: Follow-up with Dr. Efobi. Still experiencing "mini strokes." Prescribed Neurontin 300mg. EEG was normal.
  • Had been on gabapentin for approximately 4 days before the February 17 incident.

Events of February 17, 2016

  • Was driving a truck for work when episodes recurred between 0800-0830. Boss called 911.
  • EMS reported speech slightly slurred.
  • Arrived at StoneCrest ER at 0948, triaged at 0956.
  • Neurologically normal from 1000-1200 per six serial neuro checks by Nurse Bromley.
  • New neurological deficits appeared between 1200-1300: slurred speech, right-sided facial weakness, dysphasia, right arm weakness.
  • NIHSS score of 4 at StoneCrest; deteriorated to NIHSS 13 by transfer to Centennial at 2250.

Outcome

  • CT perfusion at Centennial (Feb 18) showed large left MCA stroke.
  • Discharged home Feb 26, 2016 on clopidogrel, aspirin, atorvastatin, gabapentin, lisinopril.
  • Re-admitted Feb 27 after fall at home. NIHSS worsened to 21.
  • MRI Feb 28 showed deep left hemisphere stroke with no new restricted diffusion.
  • Discharged Mar 3 to St. Thomas Midtown Rehab.
  • Left with significant cognitive and physical disabilities.

Defendant

Role in Case

Attending Emergency Medicine physician at StoneCrest Medical Center who treated John Ruffino on February 17, 2016. Primary defendant accused of medical malpractice for failure to administer IV tPA and failure to transfer the patient to a comprehensive stroke center.

Key Facts

  • First saw Mr. Ruffino at approximately 1220 on February 17, 2016.
  • Physical exam at ~1220 found: patient oriented x3, slow/slurred speech, right hand weaker than left, dysphasia, right arm weakness, alert, pupils ERL, GCS 15.
  • Recognized the speech abnormality could be a neurological change due to ischemic stroke.
  • Documented primary impression by 1411 as TIA/CVA Syndrome, Acute. Selected "Stroke/CVA" template for his note.
  • Ordered CT angiogram at 1252 for "Neurological Deficit." Ordered aspirin and MRI brain at 1414.
  • Requested neurology consult. Communicated with consulting neurologist Dr. Chitturi.
  • Testified he does not remember and "truly cannot state" what Nurse Bromley told him about Mr. Ruffino's history.
  • His note documented: "no tpa recommended by dr chitturi."
  • Added addendum at 1608 documenting discussions with Dr. Franklin/Chitturi about transfer to higher level of care.

Dispute

Plaintiffs' experts allege Dr. Archer fell below the standard of care by failing to initiate tPA and arrange transfer to a comprehensive stroke center after identifying neurological deficits at ~1220. Defendants argue the patient was outside the treatment window.
